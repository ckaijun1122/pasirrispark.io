|Pasir Ris Park|

A Day Outdoors

Unlike most of the other National Parks in Singapore, Pasir Ris Park
(Not to be confused with Pasir Ris Town Park)
is a beach park located in the eastern part of Singapore. It covers an area of 70 hectares (170 acres), and was partially built on reclaimed land.

The park's name was derived from the Malay word 'Pasir Ris', which means 'beach bolt-rope', implying a narrow beach.

Pasir Ris Park is a wonderful choice to spend time outdoors in Singapore. There are plenty of fun activities to do along the long park and it is a popular choice for outings and short, in-country vacation.

Even if you intend to stay out of the water, Pasir Ris Park is still perfect for a day of fun and exploration!
Here are some of the things you can do



| Things to do |

Cycling & Skating

As with all parks, cycling and skating is a great way to appreciate the scenery
and it is a great form of exercise.

Bring your own bikes/skates or rent one from of the Pasir Ris bike kiosk.
Check out this article to find out where you can rent a bike/skate around the park.

Camping/Picnic

Pasir Ris Park is a great location for a nice lunch/an overnight outdoor adventure.
Have a calm and peaceful lunch along the beach or sleep under the starry
night sky and wake up to the sounds of the birds chirping and the smell of the sea.

There are two areas - Area 1 & Area 3 - where camping is allowed.

However, please remember to apply for a camping permit at any AXS station before getting all "campy"!

Barbecue

Along the long park, there are almost 70 barbecue pits!

Nothing beats a great barbecue session amidst the lush greenery with a seaview
to catch up with friends & family ina casual and relaxed setting.

Don't forget to bring along your own barbecue essentials and food
when you come here for a barbecue.

Go on and book a barbecue pit online and enjoy your day off!

Mangrove Boardwalk

Pasir Ris Park also consist of a carefully preserved six-hectare mangrove forest.

The mangroves are home to many species of animals & fish
and it is an essential part of our eco-system.

Take an educational stroll along the Mangrove Boardwalk
and look out for unique plants & exotic animals.

*Note that no cycling is allowed on the boardwalk for safety reasons*

Gallop Stable

One of the more interesting thing to do at Pasir Ris Park is horse related activities.

Horse related activities such as riding lessons, pony rides and feeding,
trail rides, riding camps and many more for everyone, young and old to enjoy!

Pasir Ris Park is home to the pioneering branch of Gallop Stables
which started from humble beginnings in 2003.

If you ever want to try to ride horses/ponies without
having to worry about joining a riding club,
Pasir Ris Park is the place to go!

Check out Gallop Stables website for more information.
